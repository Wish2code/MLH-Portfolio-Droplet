Fermilab Research Intern

Project: Leveraging Generative Machine Learning for General-Purpose Calibrations in the CMS Experiment

Worked with Dr. Spandan Mondal on research applying state-of-the-art generative machine learning techniques to high-energy physics problems within the CMS experiment at CERN.

The research focused on developing and evaluating modern generative models—including diffusion models, Conditional Flow Matching, normalizing flows, CycleGANs, and optimal transport methods—to improve the calibration of simulated Large Hadron Collider collision data using real detector observations.
